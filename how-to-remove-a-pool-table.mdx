## **Preparing for the Removal**

Before you begin the removal process, gather the necessary tools and equipment. You will need a socket wrench, screwdrivers, a staple puller, moving blankets, bubble wrap, packing tape, and a dolly. Additionally, ensure you have safety gear, including gloves and safety goggles.

## **Disassembling the Pool Table**

**Removing the Pockets**

Start by removing the pockets. Use a screwdriver to detach them from the table's frame. Keep the screws and pockets together for easy reassembly.

**Disassembling the Rails and Cushions** 

Carefully remove the rails and cushions. These are typically attached with screws or bolts. Store the hardware in a bag or container.

**Taking Apart the Frame & Slate**

Unscrew the frame from the slate. Depending on your table, this may require multiple people due to the weight of the slate. Slates can weigh up to 250lbs or even more. Once detached, carefully move the slate to a secure location. 

This is the hardest part of the process. If you are not comfortable with this step, consider hiring a professional to remove the slate.

### **Protecting and Stroing Components**

Wrap the rails, cushions, and frame in moving blankets to protect them from scratches and damage during transportation. Secure the blankets with packing tape. The slate should be protected with bubble wrap and secured in a sturdy crate to prevent cracking.

Choose a storage location that is dry and climate-controlled to prevent warping or other damage to the table's components.

### **Reassembling the Pool Table**

After transporting the table components to their new location, it's time to reassemble the pool table. Begin by reattaching the slate to the frame. Carefully align and secure it using the appropriate screws.

Next, reattach the rails and cushions, making sure they are properly aligned and secure. Finally, reattach the pockets. Take your time to ensure everything is correctly put back together.

### **Maintenance and Care Tips**

To keep your pool table in top condition, regularly clean the felt and maintain the frame and rails. If the felt becomes worn or damaged, consider re-felting the table to ensure a smooth playing surface.
